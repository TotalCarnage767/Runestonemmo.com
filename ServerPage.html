The Tale of Runestone Vale

In the shadow of ancient mountains, where forgotten halls lie buried beneath frost, root, and stone, there stands a realm whispered of in caravan songs and dwarven records alike — Runestone Vale. Long before settlers arrived, the land was marked by old runes half-swallowed by earth and moss, pulsing with a quiet power that refused to die.

When those runes stirred, the Vale changed with them. Forests deepened. Ore veins seemed to breathe beneath the rock. Ruined ground remembered old paths. Travelers began to arrive — some seeking fortune, some mastery, some legend. Now the Vale stands on the edge of a new age, shaped not by a scripted story alone, but by the players who enter it.

Whether you carve tunnels beneath the mountain, master the professions of the wild, build trade and settlement, or simply wander in search of your place in the realm, your journey becomes part of the living history of the Vale.

What Runestone Vale Is

Runestone Vale is the flagship world built to showcase what RunestoneMMO can become: a modular custom MMORPG foundation for Paper servers, centered around persistent progression, handcrafted systems, and fantasy-world identity.

The realm is powered first by RunestoneCore, the platform layer responsible for economy, player systems, combat scaffolding, scoreboards, moderation, travel, claims, towns, and wider infrastructure. It is then expanded through RunestoneSkills, which brings professions, gathering, crafting, exploration, and skill progression into the world.

In short: Runestone Vale is the live world, and RunestoneMMO is the engine forging it.

Why Runestone Vale

Runestone Vale is where the systems of RunestoneMMO are tested, lived in, balanced, and shaped into a real world. It is both a playable server and a proof of vision — a place where custom mechanics are meant to feel cohesive, immersive, and worth investing time into.

Rather than throwing disconnected features together, the goal is to forge a realm where economy, progression, professions, travel, territory, and future content all belong to the same world and the same identity.
